## 1. LINKEDIN

### Source: SocialInsider LinkedIn Benchmarks 2026 (1.3M business posts analyzed)

**Engagement rates by format (2024 → 2025, by impressions):**

| Format | Avg Eng Rate 2024 | Avg Eng Rate 2025 | YoY Change |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Native document (carousel/PDF) | 6.10% | 7.00% | +14% |
| Multi-image | 6.60% | 6.45% | -2% |
| Video | 5.60% | 6.00% | +7% |
| Image (single) | 4.85% | 5.30% | +9% |
| Text | 4.00% | 4.50% | +12% |
| Poll | 4.40% | 4.20% | -5% |
| Link | 3.30% | 3.25% | -2% |

**Key finding:** Native documents lead ALL formats at 7.00% engagement — this is the **carousel/PDF engagement multiplier: 1.56x vs text posts** (7.00% / 4.50%).

**Document vs link posts: 2.15x engagement multiplier** (7.00% / 3.25%).

**LinkedIn overall average engagement rate: 5.20%** (8% YoY increase).

### Video Views (Declining)

| Page Followers | Avg Views 2025 | Avg Views 2026 | YoY Change |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| 1–5K | 190 | 155 | -18% |

**Key finding:** Video views declined **36% YoY on average** across all page sizes. LinkedIn is NOT a video-first platform despite investing in video features. Expert quote: "LinkedIn was built around professional knowledge sharing: text posts, insights, documents, frameworks."

### Dwell Time & Algorithm Correlation

- LinkedIn's algorithm rewards dwell time (time spent on post). Carousels/documents inherently drive higher dwell time due to swipe-through behavior.
- Expert (Ivy Mullins, Born Social): "Each slide invites the user to discover what comes next... This sense of discovery keeps them engaged for longer."
- Expert (Julia Holmqvist, Semrush): "Document posts perform well because they behave like 'free value'... downloadable, saveable... The carousel format makes it easy to scan."

### Save Rate

- No direct "save rate" metric published in 2024–2026 studies, but expert commentary confirms documents/carousels are "downloadable... People can save them and actually use them later (templates, checklists, frameworks)." This behavior is a strong proxy for save intent.

### Posting Frequency Trends

- "Over 2025, brands have increased their posting frequency for visual-first content formats" — SocialInsider executive summary.

## 2. X/TWITTER

### Buffer Research (Foundational, Updated Through 2026)

**Image tweets vs text-only:**
- Tweets with images earn **150% more retweets** and **18% more clicks** than text-only tweets (Buffer original research, confirmed through 2024+)
- Source: https://buffer.com/resources/twitter-images/

**Twitter's own data (verified user study, 2M+ tweets):**
- Photos: **+35% boost in retweets**
- Videos: **+28% boost**
- Quotes: **+19% boost**
- Numbers: **+17% boost**
- Hashtags: **+16% boost**

**Sotrender analysis (500 most-followed brand profiles):**
- Tweets with pic.twitter.com images: **+141% more retweets**
- Tweets with Instagram links: **-52% fewer retweets** (non-native images penalized)
- Tweets with Facebook links: **-19% fewer retweets**

**Dan Zarrella analysis (400,000+ random tweets):**
- Tweets with pic.twitter.com images: **94% more likely to be retweeted**
- Tweets with Facebook/Instagram links: **47%/42% less likely** to be retweeted

### Link-Tweet Penalty (2024–2026)

- X/Twitter has increasingly suppressed link-containing tweets in the algorithm. Multiple creator reports confirm **30–50% reduced impressions** for tweets containing external links (vs image-only or text-only).
- Workaround: post image/text first, add link in reply. This is widely documented creator behavior.
- No formal quantitative study with exact penalty magnitude available from X itself.

### Image Threads vs Text Threads

- No large-scale quantitative study found comparing image-thread vs text-thread performance specifically. Creator consensus: image threads (with custom graphics per tweet) outperform plain text threads on impressions, but data is anecdotal.

## 3. MEDIUM

### Algorithm & Distribution (2024–2026)

**What we know (from Medium's distribution standards + creator ecosystem):**

- Medium's algorithm weights **read ratio** (% of article actually read) as a primary signal.
- **Hero image:** Medium's editor auto-generates social preview cards from the hero image. Articles without a hero image receive a generic gray card in feeds, significantly reducing CTR from social shares and Medium's own recommendation feeds.
- **Image-rich articles:** Medium's curation team and algorithm do not explicitly penalize text-only articles, but articles with relevant visuals tend to have higher read ratios (users stay longer on pages with visual breaks).
- **Curation eligibility:** Medium's nominated/curated system (replaced by Boost in 2024+) does not require images, but curated articles overwhelmingly include them. Community observation: articles with 1 image per ~300 words perform well.
- **2024 Boost program:** prioritizes member engagement (claps, highlights, responses). Visual quality is not an explicit Boost criterion, but visual articles correlate with higher engagement signals.

### Quantitative Gap

- No published large-scale study comparing image-rich vs text-only Medium articles with controlled engagement metrics (2024–2026). Available data is mostly creator-anecdotal.
- Medium does not publish internal algorithm weightings.

## 4. SUBSTACK

### Notes with Images vs Text-Only

- Substack Notes (launched 2023, iterated 2024–2025) is a social feed feature. Notes with images receive algorithmically boosted feed placement per multiple creator reports, but **no quantitative study with sample sizes** has been published by Substack or third parties.
- Hootsuite Social Trends 2026 report specifically calls out Substack as "no longer just a newsletter platform — it's a true social media platform complete with a social feed, inbox, and profiles."

### Hero Image Impact on Open Rate

- Email open rates are primarily driven by subject line, not hero image (image loads after open).
- However, hero image impacts **CTR from inbox preview** in email clients that show preview images (Gmail, Apple Mail).
- No Substack-specific quantitative study published. General email marketing benchmarks (Mailchimp, HubSpot) show emails with images get **42% higher CTR** than text-only emails (older data, not Substack-specific).

### 2024–2026 Product Changes

- Substack added: Notes improvements, custom publication homepages, video support, podcast integration, mobile app upgrades.
- The platform is increasingly visual-capable but still fundamentally text/newsletter-first.

## 5. CROSS-PLATFORM BENCHMARKS

### Buffer 2026 State of Social Media Engagement Report

- **Dataset:** 52 million posts analyzed across Instagram, Facebook, Threads, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, X.
- Key finding: Platform-specific strategies required — no one-size-fits-all approach.
- LinkedIn best posting: 3–6 PM, Wed–Fri (shifted to later in day vs previous years — audiences now engaging after work hours).

### Sprout Social 2025 Content Benchmarks Report

- **Dataset:** 3 billion messages across 1 million active public profiles.
- Average brands publish 9.5 posts/day across networks (slight dip from 2023 — "focus on quality over volume").
- Average inbound engagements grew 20% YoY (2023→2024).
- Per-post engagements grew 17% YoY.
- Instagram and Facebook lead in engagement volume; TikTok emerging.

### Hootsuite Social Trends 2026

- Key theme: "Attention is the most valuable commodity — and the scarcest."
- Brands moving away from "overly polished" content → authenticity signals (imperfections, natural pacing).
- AI content now exceeds human-written content online (2025 milestone via Graphite).
- Nearly 1/3 of consumers less likely to choose brands using AI-generated ads.
- Employee advocacy: employees trusted more than influencers or CEOs (Edelman Trust Barometer 2025).
- LinkedIn's increasingly youthful audience + new video features = increased visual opportunities.

### HubSpot Visual Content Marketing Statistics (Updated 2026)

- Video is #1 media format for ROI — 50% of marketers use it.
- Images used by 47% of marketers.
- **People following directions with text+illustrations do 323% better** than text-only directions (Springer).
- Original graphics outperform stock photos (Venngage survey).
- 71% of B2B marketers say content marketing became more important in the last year (CMI).
- On Instagram, carousel posts have highest engagement of all post types (Statista).

## 8. DEVELOPER/TECHNICAL AUDIENCE

### Do Technical Audiences Respond Differently?

**GitHub Blog:** GitHub's own blog is highly visual — uses diagrams, screenshots, architecture visuals, code screenshots with syntax highlighting. Their most shared posts typically include visual explanations of features.

**dev.to:** The dev.to platform favors text-heavy technical content, but top-performing posts often include:
- Custom header/hero images (stand out in feed)
- Code screenshots over raw code blocks (for social sharing)
- Architecture diagrams
- Mermaid/PlantUML diagrams

**Hacker News (HN) audience behavior:**
- HN is fundamentally text-first — link aggregator with minimal visual UI.
- HN users click through to articles; visual content in the article helps retention but the HN submission itself is text-only.
- HN audience values **substance over aesthetics** — flashy visuals without technical depth are criticized.
- However, well-designed technical diagrams, architecture charts, and data visualizations are consistently praised and shared.

### Key Insight for Developer Audiences

- **Diagrams > decorative images.** Developer audiences respond to functional visuals (architecture diagrams, flowcharts, data charts, code screenshots) but are skeptical of purely decorative/marketing imagery.
- **Text-heavy is fine IF structured well.** Developers will read long text if it's well-organized with headers, bullet points, and code blocks.
- **Visual-first works for devs WHEN visuals carry information.** A diagram that explains a concept faster than 500 words of text is highly valued.
- No quantitative study specifically measuring developer engagement with visual vs text content was found.

## ROI Estimate for 3-Part Blog Series (Visual-First)

### Assumptions
- Current blog promotion: text-only LinkedIn posts + text tweets linking to blog
- Visual-first upgrade: LinkedIn carousel summarizing each blog post + image-tweet threads + Medium articles with inline visuals

### Conservative Estimates (based on documented data)

**LinkedIn:**
- Current text+link posts: ~3.25% engagement rate (link format)
- Visual-first carousel: ~7.00% engagement rate
- **Expected engagement lift: 2.15x** (from link to document format)
- For 10–50K follower page: impressions go from ~340/post (link) to ~1,150/post (document) = **3.4x impression lift**

**X/Twitter:**
- Text tweets linking to blog: baseline
- Image tweets (no link in main tweet, link in reply): **+35–150% retweet boost, +18% clicks**
- Conservative: **+50% overall engagement lift** on blog promotion tweets

**Medium:**
- Adding inline visuals (1 per ~300 words, custom diagrams): expected read ratio improvement of ~15–25% (directional, not hard data)

### 3-Part Series Projected Impact
- If each blog post currently reaches ~1,000 people via social: visual-first approach likely reaches **2,000–3,500** for the same content
- Total series reach: **6,000–10,500** vs **3,000** baseline = **2–3.5x total reach improvement**
- Investment: ~2–4 hours additional per post for visual asset creation (carousel design, custom diagrams, image-tweet graphics)

## Key Caveats for B2B / Developer Audiences

1. **Developer audiences are NOT the same as general B2B audiences.** Devs value substance over polish. Decorative stock imagery can actively harm credibility with technical readers.

2. **Functional visuals win with devs; decorative visuals lose.** Architecture diagrams, flowcharts, data visualizations, and annotated code screenshots are high-value. Generic "person at computer" stock photos are low/negative-value.

3. **LinkedIn carousel/document format works well for developer B2B** because devs actively seek frameworks, checklists, and reference materials — the exact content that documents deliver.

4. **HN/Reddit technical communities are text-first.** Visual-first content strategies don't map directly to link-aggregator audiences. The blog itself should have visuals; the submission/post is text.

5. **Video is declining on LinkedIn** (-36% views YoY). For developer B2B, static visual formats (carousels, diagrams, infographics) are safer bets than video.

6. **Quality bar is higher for B2B thought leadership.** 65% of decision-makers say volume of thought leadership makes quality harder to find. Visuals must enhance, not replace, substantive content.

7. **Cross-platform visual formatting is non-trivial.** Carousels that work on LinkedIn don't transfer to X/Twitter. Each platform needs native visual assets.

8. **No hard data exists for Substack/Medium visual impact.** Claims about visual-first benefits for these platforms should be marked as directional/best-practice rather than data-driven.
