That number on slide 1 — “120x” — is the cost spread between the cheapest and most expensive GitHub Copilot model. Same prompt. Same result quality. 120x different cost.

Starting June 1, 2026, that spread becomes your team’s billing reality.

The data:

▸ GPT-4.1 / GPT-5 mini: 0x (FREE on paid plans — today)
▸ Claude Haiku 4.5: 1x
▸ Claude Sonnet 4.5: 5x
▸ GPT-5.4: 20x
▸ Claude Opus 4.6: 30x
▸ RouteLLM (LMSYS, 2024): 95% GPT-4 quality at 75% lower cost
▸ Apple ML Research (2025): reasoning models burn extra tokens on simple tasks with zero quality improvement

The fix — 3-tier model routing:

1. Tier 1 (60-70% of tasks): autocomplete, variable renames, docstrings — 0x models, $0
2. Tier 2 (20-30%): code review, refactoring, docs — 1-5x, contained spend
3. Tier 3 (5-10%): architecture, security, system design — premium only when genuinely needed

Case study: 100-developer team applied this framework.
Before routing: $3,000/day
After routing: $970/day
Annual savings: $740,000 (68% reduction)

No quality sacrifice. They stopped sending variable renames to Claude Opus.

The window to audit before June 1 is closing. First action: switch your default Copilot model to GPT-4.1 today. It's free on paid plans. For most of your daily tasks, you won’t notice the difference.
